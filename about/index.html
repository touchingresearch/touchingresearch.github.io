About Me

Both as a person and as a researcher I have been fascinated by people’s need to feel connected to others. As an experimental psychologist, my ambition is to understand this basic human phenomenon and to develop interventions to help reduce feelings of isolation among vulnerable groups in society.

I started researching this topic in 2012, when I obtained my dual Research Master’s degree in Social & Organisational Psychology and Cognitive Neuroscience at Leiden University. In that same year, I received a grant from the NWO (Netherlands Organization for Scientific Research) to start my own line of research at the VU University in Amsterdam. I am now working towards a PhD at the Clinical, Neuro and Developmental Psychology Department. At this department, I am part of the Amsterdam Emotion Regulation Lab led by Professor Sander Koole.

This research has led me to develop broad, multidisciplinary interests in developmental psychology, social psychology, clinical psychology, social and affective neuroscience, and other fields. After all, early childhood experiences with our caregivers build the brain architecture that forms the foundation for later relationships, health and well-being. I also use a multi-method approach including correlational studies, experimental lab and field studies, and physiological measures such as electroencephalography (EEG), electrocardiagram (ECG), skin conductance and cortisol. My specific research interests include touch, attachment, loneliness, social emotion regulation, mental health, and existential concerns. You can read more about my work on the Research page.
